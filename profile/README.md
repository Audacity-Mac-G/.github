## 📖 Overview

<a href="#audacity">Audacity</a> is the world's most widely used free audio editor — a full-featured multi-track recording and editing application for Mac that covers recording, editing, noise reduction, effects processing, analysis, and export across all common audio formats without any cost.

The <a href="#audacity">multi-track recording and editing</a> captures audio on multiple independent tracks simultaneously and provides the complete editing toolkit — cut, copy, paste, trim, split, join, silence, duplicate — with waveform display at any zoom level from the full project to individual samples. Tracks edit independently and mix down to a final output, supporting the layered audio workflows of podcast production, voiceover recording, music demo creation, and any project requiring multiple audio sources combined. The <a href="#audacity">noise reduction tool</a> is among Audacity's most practically valuable built-in effects — analyzing a selected noise profile sample from the recording (a section containing only background noise) and subtracting that noise signature from the entire recording. HVAC hum, room fan noise, electrical hiss, and other consistent background sounds that home and office recording environments produce respond well to Audacity's noise reduction, transforming acceptable-but-noisy recordings into professional-sounding audio.

The <a href="#audacity">100-plus built-in effects library</a> covers the complete range of audio processing needs — equalization with a full parametric EQ, dynamic processing with compressor and limiter, time-based effects including reverb and delay, modulation effects, pitch correction, distortion, and dozens of specialized audio processes. Effects chains apply automatically to multiple files for consistent batch processing. The <a href="#audacity">spectral analysis and visualization</a> displays audio content as a frequency spectrogram alongside the waveform view — identifying problem resonances, characterizing noise signatures, and enabling the targeted frequency-domain editing that waveform display alone can't support.

<a href="#audacity">VST plugin support</a> loads third-party VST effects processors within Audacity — the same plugins used in commercial digital audio workstations available in Audacity's free environment, extending the built-in effects library with commercial and community-contributed processing. <a href="#audacity">MIDI track import and export</a> handles MIDI data alongside audio tracks for projects combining recorded audio with MIDI sequence information.

<a href="#audacity">Label tracks and chapter markers</a> place named position markers at specific points in the audio — annotating podcast chapters, marking edit points for review, and creating the structural reference points that organize long-form audio editing sessions. <a href="#audacity">Export format flexibility</a> outputs MP3, WAV, FLAC, OGG Vorbis, AIFF, and other formats with configurable quality and codec settings, with all formats at no additional cost through the free open-source model.
